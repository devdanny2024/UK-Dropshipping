We're upgrading UK2ME so your customers always know when their order will arrive, can sort out any problem easily, and can see the full picture before they pay. This overview explains what's changing in everyday language — the technical plan lives in the separate developer work plan.

2. How the delivery timing works

Every order travels in three simple stages. The system adds them up (and lands on the right Thursday) to give an arrival window:

StageWhat happensStandardExpress UK only
1. To our hubThe shop sends the item to our UK or US warehouse3–5 days1–2 days
2. Despatch dayWe ship out every Thursday from the UK & USWeekly — Thursdays
3. To LagosFrom our warehouse to Nigeria5–10 working days2–3 working days
Example — A UK customer orders on a Monday (standard speed). The item reaches our UK hub by mid-week, makes that Thursday's despatch, and arrives in Lagos roughly 1–2 weeks later. If the item would miss Thursday's cut-off, the system simply quotes the following Thursday — honestly, up front.

All these timings are adjustable by your team from the admin settings, so if despatch days or typical shipping times change, we update them without needing new software.

3. Disputes & refunds, step by step

  1. The customer opens an order and taps "Report a problem / Request a refund", choosing a reason and adding details.
  2. You and the customer both get an email confirming the issue is logged.
  3. Your team reviews it in admin and marks it under review, then either resolves (with a refund if agreed) or declines with a note.
  4. The customer is emailed at each step. When a refund is approved, you send the money the usual way and the order is marked refunded for a clean record.

4. The rest of this upgrade (recap)

Alongside the delivery and dispute features, this phase also delivers what we agreed earlier:

  • Separate UK (£) and US ($) baskets — customers shop one region at a time, with the right currency and rules.
  • Staff-built invoices you approve and pay — the order stays pending until the customer pays, and the customer sees a clear cost breakdown.
  • An alert to your team the moment an order is placed, including the product link, so you can review it quickly.

5. Roughly how long

The delivery dates, delivery note, and dispute/refund features add about one to one-and-a-half weeks of work on top of the core upgrade. The delivery date calculator can be built straight away, in parallel with the rest.
